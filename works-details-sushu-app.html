Sushu House App

Visit online

My role in the project
UX designer designing an app for Sushi restaurant from research to prototype.

SushuHouse is a small sushi restaurant in a big city. Their customers are mostly busy people who want to order the food online and get it delivered to avoid long waiting times in restaurants.

The problem
Many people in Greece face hunger each day, the Food Bank organization has identified the need for food relief for people and families with low-income.

Project goal
Design an online Food Delivery App for Sushi restaurant.

Target audience
Busy people all ages who want to avoid long waiting times at the restaurants.

Key challenges or constraints
Busy people who want to avoid long waiting times in the restaurants.
Shopping cart icon is missing, checkout errors.
Heavily use of images and graphics.

Usability study findings
Findings from the usability study helped me first add the option for the users to be able to place an order and after to refine the steps (in the wireframes) user takes in order to reduce the amount of time it takes to complete the order.

User research summary
I conducted interviews and created empathy maps to understand the users needs. A primary user group identified through research was busy people all ages who want to avoid long waiting times at the restaurants.
Also, research revealed that not only the long waiting times in the restaurants are the reasons people prefer the online delivery option but there are also young people, mostly students who don’t know cooking.

Takeaways
While I was designing the SushuHouse app, I learned how important is the research study in every step of the product design.
